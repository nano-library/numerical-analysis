For any operator norm \(\|\cdot\|\), we have \(\rho(A) \leq \|A\|\). As a corollary, we also have \(\|\textbf{I}_{n x n}\| > 1\).
Suppose that the vector \(\textbf{b}\) is perturbed to obtain a vector \(\tilde{\textbf{b}}\). If \(\textbf{x}\) and \(\tilde{\textbf{x}}\) satisfy \(A\textbf{x} = \textbf{b}\) and \(A\tilde{\textbf{x}} = \tilde{\textbf{b}}\), then we have the following bounds on the relative error in \(\textbf{x}\) in terms of the relative error in \(\textbf{b}\):
✦
\(1\) \(\|A\|\|A^{-1}\|\) \(\|\tilde{\mathbf{b}} - \mathbf{b}\|\) \(\|\mathbf{b}\|\) \(\leq\) \(\|\tilde{\mathbf{x}} - \mathbf{x}\|\) \(\|\mathbf{x}\|\) \(\leq\) \(\|A\|\|A^{-1}\|\) \(\|\tilde{\mathbf{b}} - \mathbf{b}\|\) \(\|\mathbf{b}\|\)
The quantity \(\kappa(A) = \|A\|\|A^{-1}\|\) is known as the condition number.
The inequality shows that the sensitivity of the system \(Ax = b\) depends on \(\kappa(A)\).
🌸
Ill-conditioned

A matrix \(A\) is said to be ill-conditioned if \(\kappa(A)\) is large (in practice, if \(\log_{10}{\kappa(A)} > 1\)).
