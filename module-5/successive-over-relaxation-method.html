The SOR method is given by, where \(\omega\) is the relaxation parameter,
✦
\(x_i^{(k+1)}\) = \((1 - \omega) x_i^{(k)}\) + \(\frac{\omega}{a_{i,i}}\) \((b_i\) - \(\displaystyle\sum_{j=1}^{i-1} a_{i,j}x_j^{(k+1)}\) - \(\displaystyle\sum_{j=i+1}^n a_{i,j}x_j^{(k)})\)
In matrix form, the method can be expressed as
✦
\(x^{(k+1)}\) = \((1-\omega)x^{(k)}\) + \(\omega D^{-1} (b - Lx^{(k+1)} - U x^{(k)})\)
✦
\(x^{(k+1)}\) = \((D + \omega L^{-1})\) + \(((1 - \omega) D - \omega U)x^{(k)}\) + \(\omega (D + \omega L)^{-1}b\)
Thus, the iteration matrix is
✦
\(H(\omega)\) = \((D + \omega L)^{-1}\) \(\left[(1 - \omega)D - \omega U\right]\)
The optimal value of w can be found as follows:
✦
\(\omega_{opt}\) = \(arg\) \(min_{\omega}\) \(\rho(H(\omega))\)
✦
\(\omega_{opt}\) \(\approx\) 2 1 + \(\sqrt{1 + \rho(H_J)^2}\)
where \(H_J\) is the iteration matrix of Gauss-Jacobi method. The value of the relaxation parameter w should fall between 0 and 2. When \(w > 1\), the method is referred to as an over relaxation method, while when \(w < 1\), the method is known as an under relaxation method.

Successive Over Relaxation

The rate of convergence of an iterative method is defined as, where \(H\) is the iteration matrix,

✦
v = \(-\ln{(\rho(H))}\)
✦
v = \(-\log_{10} {(\rho(H)})\)
The spectral radius of the SOR method is \(w_{opt} - 1\) and its rate of convergence is
✦
v = \(-\ln{(w_{opt} - 1)}\)
✦
v = \(-\log_{10} {(w_{opt} - 1)}\)
✦ Example 1 finding the optimal relaxation factor and determining the rate of convergence
Problem

The following system of equations is given:

\[ 3x + 2y = 4.5 \\[10pt] 2x + 3y - z = 5 \\[10pt] -y + 2z = 0.5 \]

Set up the SOR iteration scheme for the solution. Find the optimal relaxation factor and determine the rate of convergence.

Solution

Step 1: Write in Matrix Form \(A = L + D + U\).
The first thing we do is express the coefficient matrix \(A\) as a sum of three parts, the lower triangular \(L\), diagonal \(D\), and upper triangular \(U\).

\[ A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 2 & 0\\ 2 & 3 & -1\\ 0 & 1 & 2\\ \end{bmatrix} \]

\(L\) contains elements strictly below the diagonal,
\(D\) contains only the diagonal elements,
\(U\) contains elements strictly above the diagonal.

\[ L = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0\\ 2 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 1 & 0\\ \end{bmatrix} \\[10pt] D = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 3 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & 2\\ \end{bmatrix} \\[10pt] U = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 2 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & -1\\ 0 & 0 & 0\\ \end{bmatrix} \]

Step 2: Form the Jacobi Iteration Matrix \(H_J\).
In the Jacobi method, we isolate the diagonal part and move everything else to the right-hand side. The iteration takes the form:

\[ Dx^{(k+1)}=-(L+U)x^{(k)}+b \\[10pt] x^{(k+1)}=-D^{-1}(L+U)x^{(k)}+D^{-1}b \]

So the Jacobi iteration matrix is defined as:

\[ H_J = -D^{-1}(L+U) \]

First, compute \(D^{-1}\), for a diagonal matrix, simply take the reciprocal of each diagonal entry:

\[ D^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1/3 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 1/3 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & 1/2\\ \end{bmatrix} \]

Next, find \((L+U)\)

\[ L + U = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 2 & 0\\ 2 & 0 & -1\\ 0 & -1 & 0\\ \end{bmatrix} \]

The iteration matrix of the Gauss-Jacobi method is given by

\[ H_J = - \begin{bmatrix} 1/3 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 1/3 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & 1/2\\ \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 2 & 0\\ 2 & 0 & -1\\ 0 & -1 & 0\\ \end{bmatrix} \] \[ H_J = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 2/3 & 0\\ 2/3 & 0 & -1/3\\ 0 & -1/2 & 0\\ \end{bmatrix} \]

Step 3: Find the Eigenvalues of \(H_J\).
The spectral radius \(\rho(H_J)\), the largest absolute eigenvalue, controls how fast the iteration converges.
To find it, solve:

\[ |H_J - \lambda I| = 0 \] \[ \left| \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -2/3 & 0\\ -2/3 & 0 & 1/2\\ 0 & 1/2 & 0\\ \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} \lambda & 0 & 0\\ 0 & \lambda & 0\\ 0 & 0 & \lambda\\ \end{bmatrix} \right| = 0 \] \[ \begin{vmatrix} -\lambda & -2/3 & 0\\ -2/3 & -\lambda & 1/2\\ 0 & 1/2 & -\lambda\\ \end{vmatrix} = 0 \] \[ -\lambda (-\lambda^2 - \frac{1}{6}) - \frac{2}{3}(-\frac{2}{3}\lambda - 0) + 0 = 0 \\[10pt] - \lambda^3 + \frac{1}{6}\lambda + \frac{4}{9}\lambda = 0 \\[10pt] - \lambda^3 + \lambda \left(\frac{1}{6} + \frac{4}{9}\right) = 0 \\[10pt] - \lambda^3 + \lambda \left(\frac{9}{54} + \frac{24}{54}\right) = 0 \\[10pt] - \lambda^3 + \lambda \left(\frac{11}{18}\right) = 0 \\[10pt] - \lambda \left(\lambda^2 + \frac{11}{18}\right) = 0 \\[10pt] \]

The eigenvalues of \(H_J\) are \(\lambda = 0, \sqrt{\frac{11}{18}}, -\sqrt{\frac{11}{18}}\).
The spectral radius is the largest absolute eigenvalue

\[ \rho(H_J) = \text{max } \left(|0|, \left|\sqrt{\frac{11}{18}}\right|, \left|-\sqrt{\frac{11}{18}}\right| \right) \\[10pt] \rho(H_J) = \mu = \sqrt{\frac{11}{18}} \\[10pt] \rho(H_J) = \mu \approx 0.7454 \]

Since \(\rho < 1\), Jacobi converges for this system.
The optimal relaxation parameter for the SOR method is obtained as:

\[ \omega_{opt} = \frac{2}{1 + \sqrt{1 - \mu^2}} \\[10pt] \omega_{opt} = \frac{2}{1 + \sqrt{1 - \frac{11}{18}}} \\[10pt] \omega_{opt} \approx 1.2318 \] \[ \rho(H_{SOR}) = \omega_{opt} - 1 = 0.2318 \]

The rate of convergence of the SOR method is

\[ \text{rate } = \log_{10}{(\rho(H_{SOR}))} \\[10pt] \text{rate } = \log_{10}{(0.23183)} \\[10pt] \text{rate } \approx 0.6348 \]
✦ Example 2 finding the optimal relaxation factor and determining the rate of convergence
Problem

Suppose the system of equations \(Ax = b\) is to be solved with

\[ A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & k\\ 2k & 1\\ \end{bmatrix}, \; k \neq \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}, \; \text{ k is real} \]
a Find a necessary and sufficient condition on \(k\) for convergence of the Gauss-Jacobi method.
b For \(k = 0.25\), determine the optimal relaxation factor \(\omega\), if the system is to be solved with relaxation method.
Solution

Part (a): Necessary & Sufficient Condition for Jacobi Convergence
Step 1: Write in Matrix Form \(A = L + D + U\)

The first thing we do is express the coefficient matrix \(A\) as a sum of three parts, the lower triangular \(L\), diagonal \(D\), and upper triangular \(U\).

\[ A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & k\\ 2k & 1\\ \end{bmatrix} \]

\(L\) contains elements strictly below the diagonal,
\(D\) contains only the diagonal elements,
\(U\) contains elements strictly above the diagonal.

\[ L = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0\\ 2k & 0\\ \end{bmatrix} \\[10pt] D = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0\\ 0 & 1\\ \end{bmatrix} \\[10pt] U = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & k\\ 0 & 0\\ \end{bmatrix} \]

Step 2: Form the Jacobi Iteration Matrix \(H_J\).
In the Jacobi method, we isolate the diagonal part and move everything else to the right-hand side. The iteration takes the form:

\[ Dx^{(k+1)}=-(L+U)x^{(k)}+b \\[10pt] x^{(k+1)}=-D^{-1}(L+U)x^{(k)}+D^{-1}b \]

So the Jacobi iteration matrix is defined as:

\[ H_J = -D^{-1}(L+U) \]

First, compute \(D^{-1}\), for a diagonal matrix, simply take the reciprocal of each diagonal entry:

\[ D^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0\\ 0 & 1\\ \end{bmatrix} \]

Next, find \((L+U)\)

\[ L + U = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & k\\ 2k & 0\\ \end{bmatrix} \]

The iteration matrix of the Gauss-Jacobi method is given by

\[ H_J = - \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0\\ 0 & 1\\ \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0 & k\\ 2k & 0\\ \end{bmatrix} \] \[ H_J = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -k\\ -2k & 0\\ \end{bmatrix} \]

Step 3: Find the Eigenvalues of \(H_J\).
The spectral radius \(\rho(H_J)\), the largest absolute eigenvalue, controls how fast the iteration converges.
To find it, solve:

\[ |H_J - \lambda I| = 0 \\[10pt] \left| \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -k\\ -2k & 0\\ \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} \lambda & 0\\ 0 & \lambda\\ \end{bmatrix} \right| = 0 \\[10pt] \begin{vmatrix} -\lambda & -k\\ -2k & -\lambda\\ \end{vmatrix} = 0 \\[10pt] \lambda^2 - 2k^2 = 0 \\[10pt] \lambda = \pm \sqrt{2} |k| \]

Step 4: Apply convergence condition.
The Jacobi method converges if and only if the spectral radius is less than 1:

\[ \rho(H_J) < 1 \\[10pt] \sqrt{2} |k| < 1 \\[10pt] |k| < \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \\[10pt] -\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} < k <\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \]

Part (b): Optimal Relaxation Factor for \(k = 0.25\)
Step 1: Compute Spectral Radius at \(k = 0.25\)

\[ \rho(H_J) = \mu = \sqrt{2}|k|\\[10pt] \rho(H_J) = \sqrt{2}(0.25) \approx 0.3536 \]

Since \(\mu = 0.3536\) < 1, system converges with Jacobi.
Step 2: The optimal relaxation parameter for the SOR method is obtained as:

\[ \omega_{opt} = \frac{2}{1 + \sqrt{1 - \mu^2}} \\[10pt] \omega_{opt} = \frac{2}{1 + \sqrt{1 - (0.3536)}} \\[10pt] \omega_{opt} \approx 1.0333 \] \[ \rho(H_{SOR}) = \omega_{opt} - 1 = 0.0333 \]

The rate of convergence of the SOR method is

\[ \text{rate } = \log_{10}{(\rho(H_{SOR}))} \\[10pt] \text{rate } = \log_{10}{(0.0333)} \\[10pt] \text{rate } \approx 1.4775 \]
