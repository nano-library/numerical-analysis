✨ The objective is to determine the best fit function of the form \(f (x; a)\) for a given set of data points \((x_i, y_i), i = 0, 1, \ldots , m,\) where \(a = (a_0, a_1, \ldots , a_n)\) is a vector of parameters. The discrete least squares problem is to find the value of a that minimizes the least square error,
✦
\(E\) \((\) \(a_0, a_1, \ldots, a_n\) \()\) = \(E\) \((\) a \()\)
✦
\(E\) \((\) a \()\) = \(\displaystyle\sum_{\color{#6AAEE0}{i}=0}^{\color{#6AAEE0}{m}}\) \((\) \(y_i\) - \(f(x_i ; a)\) \()^{\color{#6AAEE0}{2}}\)

Linear Fit

The simplest example of the best fit function is the linear curve \(f (x; a_0, a_1) = a_0 + a_1 x. \)
✦ Least Square Error
✦
\(E\) \((\) \(a_0, a_1\) \()\) = \(\displaystyle\sum_{\color{#6AAEE0}{i}=0}^{\color{#6AAEE0}{m}}\) \((\) \(y_i\) - \((\) \(a_0 + a_1 x_i\) \()\) \()^{\color{#6AAEE0}{2}}\)
For minimum \(E\),
✦
\(\displaystyle\frac{\partial E}{\partial a_0}\) = - 2 \(\displaystyle\sum_{\color{#6AAEE0}{i}=0}^{\color{#6AAEE0}{m}}\) \((\) \(y_i\) - \((\) \(a_0 + a_1 x_i\) \()\) \()^{\color{#6AAEE0}{2}}\) = 0
✦
\(\displaystyle\frac{\partial E}{\partial a_1}\) = - 2 \(\displaystyle\sum_{\color{#6AAEE0}{i}=0}^{\color{#6AAEE0}{m}}\) \((\) \(y_i\) - \((\) \(a_0 + a_1 x_i\) \()\) \()\) \(x_i\) = 0
Simplifying, we get the following linear system
✦
\(m a_0\) + \(a_1\) 2 \(\displaystyle\sum_{\color{#6AAEE0}{i}=0}^{\color{#6AAEE0}{m}}\) \(x_i\) = \(\displaystyle\sum_{\color{#6AAEE0}{i}=0}^{\color{#6AAEE0}{m}}\) \(y_i\)
✦
\(a_0\) \(\displaystyle\sum_{\color{#6AAEE0}{i}=0}^{\color{#6AAEE0}{m}}\) \(x_i\) + \(a_1\) \(\displaystyle\sum_{\color{#6AAEE0}{i}=0}^{\color{#6AAEE0}{m}}\) \(x_i\) = \(\displaystyle\sum_{\color{#6AAEE0}{i}=0}^{\color{#6AAEE0}{m}}\) \(x_i\) \(y_i\)
Finally, \(a_0\) and \(a_1\) can be found by solving the above system.
✦ Example 1 Finding parameters
Problem

How can non-linear regression be employed to ascertain the parameters \(a\) and \(b\) in the equation \(y = ax^b\), given the data presented by an investigator as depicted in the table below?

\[ \begin{array}{c|c|c|c|c|c} x & 1 & 2 & 5 & 7 & 10 \\ \hline f(x) & 0.5 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 \end{array} \]
Solution

Step 1: Linearization. The given model is \(y = ax^b\). Taking natural logarithms:

\[ \ln y = \ln a + b \ln x \]

Let \(Y = \ln y\), \(X = \ln x\), and \(A = \ln a\). Then we have the linear form:

\[ Y = A + bX \]

Step 2: Normal equations.

\[ \begin{aligned} \sum Y &= nA + b\sum X\\ \sum XY &= A\sum X + b\sum X^2 \end{aligned} \]

Step 3: Compute the necessary sums. We construct the following table:

\[ \begin{array}{c|c|c|c|c} x & y & X = \ln x & Y = \ln y & X^2 & XY \\ \hline 1 & 0.5 & 0 & -0.6931 & 0 & 0 \\ 2 & 2 & 0.6931 & 0.6931 & 0.4805 & 0.4805 \\ 5 & 3 & 1.6094 & 1.0986 & 2.5903 & 1.7681 \\ 7 & 4 & 1.9459 & 1.3863 & 3.7866 & 2.6976 \\ 10 & 5 & 2.3026 & 1.6094 & 5.3019 & 3.7059 \\ \hline \sum & & 6.5511 & 4.0943 & 12.1592 & 8.6521 \end{array} \]

Here, \(n = 5\).
Step 4: Substitute into normal equations.

\[ \begin{aligned} 4.0943 &= 5A + 6.5511\,b \\ 8.6521 &= 6.5511\,A + 12.1592\,b \end{aligned} \]

Solving:

\[ A = -0.3858, \qquad b = 0.9194 \]

Step 5: Back-transform. Since \(A = \ln a\), we have:

\[ a = e^{A} = e^{-0.3858} = 0.6799 \]

Step 6: Final model.

\[ y = 0.6799 \, x^{0.9194} \]
✦ Example 2 Linear Regression
Problem
The following eight data points show the relationship between the number of fishermen and the amount of fish (in thousand pounds) they can catch a day. \[ \begin{array}{c|c|c|c|c|c|c|c|c} \text{Number of Fishermen} & 4 & 5 & 9 & 10 & 12 & 14 & 18 & 22 \\ \hline \text{Fish Caught} & 7 & 8 & 9 & 12 & 15 & 20 & 26 & 35 \\ \end{array} \] Using the least-squares method, compute a linear polynomial that fits the above data.
Solution

Step 1: Model formulation. We want to fit a linear polynomial of the form:

\[ y = a + bx \]

where \(x\) represents number of fishermen and \(y\) represents the fish caught (in thousand pounds).
The parameters \(a\) and \(b\) are to be determined using the least-squares method.
Step 2: Normal equations. For \(n\) data points \((x_i, y_i)\), the least-squares normal equations are:

\[ \begin{aligned} \sum y_i &= n a + b \sum x_i \\ \sum x_i y_i &= a \sum x_i + b \sum x_i^2 \end{aligned} \]

Step 3: Compute the necessary sums. We construct the following table:

\[ \begin{array}{c|c|c|c|c} x & y & x^2 & xy \\ \hline 4 & 7 & 16 & 28 \\ 5 & 8 & 25 & 40 \\ 9 & 9 & 81 & 81 \\ 10 & 12 & 100 & 120 \\ 12 & 15 & 144 & 180 \\ 14 & 20 & 196 & 280 \\ 18 & 26 & 324 & 468 \\ 22 & 35 & 484 & 770 \\ \hline \sum x = 94 & \sum y = 132 & \sum x^2 = 1370 & \sum xy = 1967 \end{array} \]

Here, \(n = 8\).
Step 4: Substitute into normal equations.

\[ \begin{aligned} 132 &= 8a + 94b \quad &(1) \\ 1967 &= 94a + 1370b \quad &(2) \end{aligned} \]

Solving:

\[ A = -1.9105, \qquad b = 1.5668 \]

Step 5: Final fitted linear model. The least-squares regression line is:

\[ y = -1.9105 + 1.5668x \]
